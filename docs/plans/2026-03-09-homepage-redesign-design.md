# Homepage Redesign Design

## Goal
Redesign the homepage to explain the product and convert visitors to signups. Playful, confident tone — witty but not campy. Think Dollar Shave Club meets Mailchimp.

## Key Constraints
- No mention of AI anywhere — the product is a "weekly family newspaper"
- No social proof/testimonials (not yet)
- No JS interactivity — pure HTML/CSS, fast loading
- Copy must be sharp, marketing-grade, humor that earns its place
- Design inspired by: Dollar Shave Club (confidence), Linear (whitespace), Superhuman (benefit-focused copy), Basecamp (simplicity)

## Sections (top to bottom)

### 1. Hero
- Title: "The Poopin' Papers"
- Tagline: "The Only Newspaper Worth Sitting Down For"
- One sharp line selling the outcome, not the product
- CTA: "Start Your First Issue" → /signup
- Price anchor: "$5/mo · Cancel anytime"

### 2. Newsletter Preview
- Real newsletter HTML rendered in a newspaper-style frame
- Slight rotation, paper shadow, tactile feel
- Hardcoded sample HTML — a well-crafted example newsletter
- Caption reinforcing value

### 3. How It Works
- 3 steps, clean horizontal layout
- Step 1: Sign up, tell us about your crew
- Step 2: Your paper shows up every week
- Step 3: Print it, hang it on the fridge, read it on the throne
- Numbered, clean, not emoji-heavy

### 4. Feature Grid
- 6 cards for section types: Meal Plans, Chores, Coaching, Fun Zone, Brain Fuel, Custom
- Each: emoji, short title, one sharp line of copy
- 3x2 grid on desktop, stacked on mobile

### 5. Pricing + Final CTA
- Centered pricing card: "$5/mo"
- 4 bullet points of what's included
- CTA: "Get Started" → /signup
- "Cancel anytime" reassurance

### 6. Footer
- "Poopin' Papers · Est. 2026"
- Minimal, on-brand
